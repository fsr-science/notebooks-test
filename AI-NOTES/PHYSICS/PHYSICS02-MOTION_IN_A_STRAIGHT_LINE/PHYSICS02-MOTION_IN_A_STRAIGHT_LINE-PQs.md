## Case 1 — Uniformly Accelerated Car 🟡

**Passage:** A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly along a straight road. After 10 s, it has covered a distance of 125 m. The driver then maintains a constant speed for 30 s and finally applies brakes, decelerating uniformly to rest in 5 s. A traffic sensor records the car passing two checkpoints 200 m apart (within the uniform-speed phase).

---

**Q51.** The acceleration of the car during the first 10 s is:

- (a) 1.25 m s⁻²
- **(b) 2.5 m s⁻²**
- (c) 5 m s⁻²
- (d) 12.5 m s⁻²

---

**Q52.** The maximum speed attained by the car is:

- (a) 12.5 m s⁻¹
- **(b) 25 m s⁻¹**
- (c) 50 m s⁻¹
- (d) 5 m s⁻¹

---

**Q53.** The deceleration during braking is:

- **(a) 5 m s⁻²**
- (b) 2.5 m s⁻²
- (c) 10 m s⁻²
- (d) 25 m s⁻²

---

**Q54.** The total distance covered by the car is:

- (a) 812.5 m
- **(b) 937.5 m**
- (c) 750 m
- (d) 1000 m

---

**Q55.** The time taken by the car to travel between the two sensor checkpoints (200 m, during uniform speed phase) is:

- (a) 4 s
- (b) 10 s
- **(c) 8 s**
- (d) 5 s

---

**Case 1 Answers:**

Q51 → **(b) 2.5 m s⁻²** : $x = \frac{1}{2}at^2$ → $125 = \frac{1}{2}a(100)$ → $a = 2.5$ m s⁻²

Q52 → **(b) 25 m s⁻¹** : $v = at = 2.5 \times 10 = 25$ m s⁻¹

Q53 → **(a) 5 m s⁻²** : $a = v/t = 25/5 = 5$ m s⁻²

Q54 → **(b) 937.5 m** : $d_1 = 125$ m; $d_2 = 25 \times 30 = 750$ m; $d_3 = \frac{1}{2}(25)(5) = 62.5$ m → total = $937.5$ m

Q55 → **(c) 8 s** : $t = 200/25 = 8$ s

## Case 3 — Relative Velocity and Overtaking 🟡🔴

---

**Q65.** The speed of train P in m s⁻¹ is:

- **(b) 20 m s⁻¹**

- (d) 30 m s⁻¹

**Q66.** The velocity of train P relative to train Q is:

- **(a) 5 m s⁻¹**

- (d) 35 m s⁻¹

**Q67.** As seen by a passenger in train P, train Q appears to move:

- (a) forward at 5 m s⁻¹
- **(b) backward at 5 m s⁻¹**

- (d) at rest

**Q68.** Time for P to completely overtake Q is:

- (a) 100 s
- **(b) 140 s**

**Case 3 Answers:**

**Q78.** *(Numerical)* A car at 90 km h⁻¹ is brought to rest in 200 m. Find (i) retardation and (ii) time taken to stop.

> [!example] Solution
> $u = 25$ m s⁻¹, $v = 0$, $s = 200$ m
>
> **(i)** $v^2 = u^2 + 2as \Rightarrow 0 = 625 + 2a(200) \Rightarrow \mathbf{a = -1.5625}$ **m s⁻²**
>
> **(ii)** $v = u + at \Rightarrow 0 = 25 - 1.5625t \Rightarrow \mathbf{t = 16}$ **s**

**Q79.** *(Numerical)* Car 1 moves at constant 20 m s⁻¹. Car 2 starts from rest with acceleration 2 m s⁻². Both start simultaneously from the same point. (i) When does Car 2 overtake Car 1? (ii) Distance from start? (iii) Speed of Car 2 at that moment?

> [!example] Solution
> **(i)** Set $x_1 = x_2$: $20t = \tfrac{1}{2}(2)t^2 \Rightarrow t(t - 20) = 0 \Rightarrow \mathbf{t = 20}$ **s**
>
> **(ii)** $d = 20 \times 20 = \mathbf{400}$ **m**
>
> **(iii)** $v_2 = 0 + 2(20) = \mathbf{40}$ **m s⁻¹** — exactly twice the speed of Car 1!

**Q80.** *(Numerical)* An object dropped from 490 m. (i) Time to reach ground? (ii) Velocity just before impact? (g = 9.8 m s⁻²)

> [!example] Solution
> **(i)** $h = \tfrac{1}{2}gt^2 \Rightarrow 490 = \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2 \Rightarrow t^2 = 100 \Rightarrow \mathbf{t = 10}$ **s**
>
> **(ii)** $v = gt = 9.8 \times 10 = \mathbf{98}$ **m s⁻¹**
