# PART B: ASSERTION–REASON QUESTIONS ⭐⭐

> [!note] Instructions for Assertion–Reason Questions Choose the correct option:
> 
> - **(a)** Both Assertion and Reason are **true**, and Reason **is** the correct explanation of Assertion.
> - **(b)** Both Assertion and Reason are **true**, but Reason is **NOT** the correct explanation of Assertion.
> - **(c)** Assertion is **true**, but Reason is **false**.
> - **(d)** Assertion is **false**, but Reason is **true**.
> - **(e)** Both Assertion and Reason are **false**.

**Q41.** 🟢 **Assertion (A):** Blood pressure in humans is greater at the feet than at the brain. **Reason (R):** Pressure in a fluid increases with the depth of the fluid column above.

---

**Q42.** 🟢 **Assertion (A):** A hydraulic lift is based on Pascal's law. **Reason (R):** Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid at rest is transmitted undiminished in all directions.

---

**Q43.** 🟡 **Assertion (A):** A spinning cricket ball in air does not follow a parabolic trajectory. **Reason (R):** Due to the Magnus effect, spinning creates unequal air velocities (and hence unequal pressures) on the two sides of the ball, resulting in a lateral force.

---

**Q44.** 🟡 **Assertion (A):** In a horizontal pipe of non-uniform cross-section, the liquid flows faster in the narrower region. **Reason (R):** At the narrower region, the pressure is lower.

---

**Q45.** 🟡 **Assertion (A):** Two soap bubbles of different sizes, when connected by a tube, cause the smaller bubble to shrink and the larger bubble to grow. **Reason (R):** Excess pressure inside a soap bubble is inversely proportional to its radius; the smaller bubble has higher internal pressure, so air flows from it into the larger bubble.

---

**Q46.** 🟡 **Assertion (A):** Viscosity of gases increases with temperature, while viscosity of liquids decreases with temperature. **Reason (R):** In liquids, rising temperature disrupts intermolecular cohesion, allowing layers to flow more easily. In gases, rising temperature increases random molecular motion, increasing momentum transfer between layers.

---

**Q47.** 🟡 **Assertion (A):** Water rises to a greater height in a capillary tube than mercury is depressed in the same tube. **Reason (R):** Surface tension of water is greater than surface tension of mercury.

---

**Q48.** 🔴 **Assertion (A):** Bernoulli's equation cannot be used to describe the flow of water through a rapid in a river. **Reason (R):** Flow through a river rapid is turbulent, and Bernoulli's equation applies only to steady, non-turbulent streamline flow.

---

**Q49.** 🔴 **Assertion (A):** Terminal velocity of a small raindrop is proportional to the square of its radius. **Reason (R):** Stokes' law gives the drag force as F = 6πηav, and at terminal velocity, drag + buoyancy = gravitational force, leading to vt = 2a²(ρ−σ)g/9η.

---

**Q50.** 🔴 **Assertion (A):** The pressure inside a soap bubble is greater than the atmospheric pressure outside it. **Reason (R):** The surface tension of the soap film creates an excess pressure ΔP = 4S/r directed inward (from outside towards inside), compressing the air inside.

**Q77.** A steel ball (radius r = 2 mm, density ρ = 8000 kg m⁻³) falls through glycerine (density σ = 1300 kg m⁻³, η = 0.83 Pa·s). Find the terminal velocity. (g = 9.8 m s⁻²)

---

### ✅ E ANSWERS

> [!note] Q73 **(a)** By Torricelli's law (depth below surface = 5 m): $v = \sqrt{2gh} = \sqrt{2 \times 10 \times 5} = \sqrt{100} = \mathbf{10\ \text{m s}^{-1}}$
> 
> **(b)** Hole is 2 m above ground. Time to fall to ground: $t = \sqrt{2h_2/g} = \sqrt{2 \times 2/10} = \sqrt{0.4} \approx 0.632\ \text{s}$
> 
> Horizontal range = $v \times t = 10 \times 0.632 = \mathbf{6.32\ \text{m}}$

> [!note] Q74 Equal pressure: $\rho_{Hg} \times g \times h_{Hg} = \rho_{water} \times g \times h_{water}$
> 
> $h_{water} = \frac{\rho_{Hg}}{\rho_{water}} \times h_{Hg} = 13.6 \times 76\ \text{cm} = \mathbf{1033.6\ \text{cm} \approx 10.34\ \text{m}}$
> 
> This shows why Torricelli used mercury, not water — a 10 m water tube is impractically tall.

> [!note] Q75 **(a)** Soap bubble has two surfaces: $\Delta P = 4S/r = 4 \times 0.04 / 0.01 = \mathbf{16\ \text{Pa}}$
> 
> **(b)** Force on one hemisphere = $\Delta P \times \pi r^2 = 16 \times \pi \times (0.01)^2 = 16\pi \times 10^{-4} \approx \mathbf{5.03 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{N}}$

> [!note] Q76 Same flow rate Q: $A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2$
> 
> $\pi r_1^2 v_1 = \pi r_2^2 v_2 \Rightarrow \frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{r_2^2}{r_1^2} = \frac{4}{1}$
> 
> **v₁ : v₂ = 4 : 1** — the narrower vessel (r₁ = 1 mm) has 4× higher velocity. (Directly relevant to blood flow in arteries — narrow arteries carry faster flow for same cardiac output.)

> [!note] Q77 $v_t = \frac{2r^2(\rho - \sigma)g}{9\eta} = \frac{2 \times (2 \times 10^{-3})^2 \times (8000 - 1300) \times 9.8}{9 \times 0.83}$
> 
> $= \frac{2 \times 4 \times 10^{-6} \times 6700 \times 9.8}{7.47} = \frac{524960 \times 10^{-6}}{7.47} \approx \mathbf{0.0703\ \text{m s}^{-1} \approx 7.0\ \text{cm s}^{-1}}$
