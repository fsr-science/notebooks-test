# 📖 CHAPTER 1 — GLOSSARY & TERMINOLOGY
> **Units and Measurement** | All terms alphabetically arranged with precise scientific definitions, dimensional formulae, and exam relevance tags.

---

## A

> [!info] **Accuracy** *(Board/NEET)*
> The degree to which a measured value agrees with the **true or accepted value** of a quantity.
> 
> > ⚠️ A measurement can be **precise but inaccurate** — all readings cluster together but away from the true value.
> 
> *Contrast with: Precision (closeness to each other, not to true value)*

---

> [!note] **Ampere (A)**
> The SI base unit of **electric current**. Defined (since 2018) by fixing the elementary charge:
> $$e = 1.602176634 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$$
> Symbol is capitalised (**A**) because named after André-Marie Ampère.

---

> [!note] **Ångström (Å)**
> A unit of length used to express atomic and molecular sizes.
> $$1 \text{ Å} = 10^{-10} \text{ m} = 0.1 \text{ nm}$$
> Not an SI unit; commonly used in spectroscopy. Hydrogen atom radius $\approx 0.5$ Å.

---

> [!note] **Arithmetic Mean** *(of measurements)*
> The average of a set of repeated measurements — used to reduce random errors.
> $$\bar{a} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n a_i}{n}$$

---

## B

> [!note] **Barn (b)**
> A unit of nuclear cross-sectional area.
> $$1 \text{ barn} = 10^{-28} \text{ m}^2 = 100 \text{ fm}^2$$
> Used in nuclear and particle physics.

---

> [!important] **Base Quantity** *(All levels)*
> A fundamental physical quantity defined independently of others. The **seven SI base quantities** are:
> 
> | # | Base Quantity | Dimension Symbol |
> |:---:|:---|:---:|
> | 1 | Length | $[L]$ |
> | 2 | Mass | $[M]$ |
> | 3 | Time | $[T]$ |
> | 4 | Electric current | $[A]$ |
> | 5 | Thermodynamic temperature | $[K]$ |
> | 6 | Amount of substance | $[\text{mol}]$ |
> | 7 | Luminous intensity | $[\text{cd}]$ |

---

> [!important] **Base Unit** *(All levels)*
> The SI unit corresponding to a base quantity. All other SI units (derived units) can be expressed in terms of these seven:
> **metre, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, candela**

---

> [!note] **BIPM** *(Bureau International des Poids et Mesures)*
> The International Bureau of Weights and Measures, located in Sèvres, France. Maintains international measurement standards and defines the SI system. Established by the Metre Convention in 1875.

---

## C

> [!note] **Candela (cd)** *(Board)*
> The SI base unit of **luminous intensity** in a given direction. Defined (since 2018) by fixing:
> $$K_{cd} = 683 \text{ lm W}^{-1} \text{ at frequency } 540 \times 10^{12} \text{ Hz}$$

---

> [!note] **CGS System**
> A system of units based on **centimetre (cm), gram (g), and second (s)** as fundamental units. Largely replaced by SI but still used in some areas of physics (e.g., electromagnetic units).
> 
> | Quantity | CGS Unit |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | Length | centimetre |
> | Mass | gram |
> | Time | second |

---

> [!note] **CGPM** *(Conférence Générale des Poids et Mesures)*
> The General Conference on Weights and Measures. Meets periodically to maintain the SI system.
> - **11th CGPM (1960)**: officially established the SI system
> - **26th CGPM (2018)**: approved redefinition of all base units using fundamental constants

---

## D

> [!important] **Derived Quantity** *(All levels)*
> A physical quantity expressed in terms of **two or more base quantities** through algebraic relationships.
> 
> | Derived Quantity | Relation |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | Velocity | length / time |
> | Force | mass × length / time² |
> | Density | mass / volume |

---

> [!important] **Derived Unit** *(All levels)*
> A unit obtained by combining base units mathematically. Some derived units have special names:
> 
> | Special Name | In Base Units |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | Newton (N) | $\text{kg m s}^{-2}$ |
> | Joule (J) | $\text{kg m}^2 \text{s}^{-2}$ |
> | Pascal (Pa) | $\text{kg m}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2}$ |

---

> [!important] **Dimensional Analysis** *(All levels — important)*
> Also called the **factor label method**. A mathematical technique using the principle of homogeneity to:
> 1. Check the **correctness** of physical equations
> 2. **Derive** relationships between physical quantities
> 3. **Convert** from one system of units to another

---

> [!note] **Dimensional Equation** *(Board/NEET/JEE)*
> An equation obtained by equating a physical quantity with its dimensional formula. Examples:
> $$[F] = [M L T^{-2}] \qquad [E] = [M L^2 T^{-2}] \qquad [\rho] = [M L^{-3} T^0]$$

---

> [!note] **Dimensional Formula** *(Board/NEET/JEE)*
> The expression showing the powers to which the seven base quantities are raised to represent a physical quantity. Written inside square brackets, e.g., $[M L^2 T^{-2}]$ for energy.

---

> [!note] **Dimensionless Quantity** *(NEET/JEE)*
> A physical quantity with $[M^0 L^0 T^0]$ (all exponents zero). Examples:
> - Plane angle (arc length / radius = $L/L$)
> - Refractive index (speed ratio)
> - Strain ($\Delta L / L$)
> - Relative density
> 
> > ⚠️ Arguments of $\sin$, $\cos$, $\log$, $\exp$ must **always** be dimensionless.

---

> [!note] **Dimensions**
> The powers (exponents) to which base quantities $[M]$, $[L]$, $[T]$, etc., are raised to represent a physical quantity. Describe the **nature** of a quantity, independent of magnitude or units.

---

## E

> [!warning] **Error (Measurement)** *(Board/NEET)*
> The difference between the measured value and the true value of a physical quantity.
> 
> | Error Type | Description | Reducible by Averaging? |
> |:---:|:---|:---:|
> | Systematic error | Consistent bias, same direction | ❌ No |
> | Random error | Unpredictable fluctuations | ✅ Yes |
> | Gross error | Blunders / misreadings | N/A |

---

## F

> [!note] **Factor Label Method**
> See *Dimensional Analysis*. Involves multiplying a measurement by unit factors (= 1) to convert between units.
> $$1 \text{ km h}^{-1} \times \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ km}} \times \frac{1 \text{ h}}{3600 \text{ s}} = \frac{5}{18} \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

---

> [!note] **Fermi (fm)**
> A unit of length used in nuclear physics. Named after Enrico Fermi.
> $$1 \text{ fm} = 10^{-15} \text{ m} = 1 \text{ femtometre}$$
> Typical atomic nucleus size $\approx 1$–$10$ fm.

---

> [!note] **FPS System**
> A system of units based on **foot (ft), pound (lb), and second (s)**. Also called the British System. Largely obsolete for scientific work.

---

## G

> [!note] **Gram (g)**
> The CGS unit of mass.
> $$1 \text{ kg} = 1000 \text{ g} = 10^3 \text{ g}$$
> Note: The SI unit of mass is the **kilogram**, not the gram.

---

## H

> [!important] **Homogeneity of Dimensions / Principle of Homogeneity** *(All levels — critical)*
> In any physically valid equation, **all terms must have the same dimensions**.
> 
> $$\text{If all terms have same dimensions} \Rightarrow \text{equation is possibly correct}$$
> $$\text{If any term has different dimensions} \Rightarrow \text{equation is definitely wrong}$$
> 
> > ⚠️ Passes test ≠ Correct. Fails test = Definitely wrong.

---

## I

> [!important] **International System of Units (SI)**
> *Système Internationale d'Unités.* The modern internationally agreed system of measurement based on **7 base units**. Established 1960 (11th CGPM), revised 2018 (26th CGPM) — all base units now defined using fundamental constants.

---

## K

> [!note] **Kelvin (K)**
> The SI base unit of **thermodynamic temperature**. Defined (since 2018) by fixing:
> $$k_B = 1.380649 \times 10^{-23} \text{ J K}^{-1}$$
> Kelvin scale starts at **absolute zero** (0 K = −273.15°C). No negative values.

---

> [!note] **Kilogram (kg)** *(Board/All)*
> The SI base unit of **mass**. Previously defined by the IPK (a physical Pt-Ir artifact). Since 2018, defined by fixing:
> $$h = 6.62607015 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$$
> Symbol is **kg** (lowercase, despite starting with a capital-seeming 'k').

---

## L

> [!info] **Least Count** *(Board/NEET)*
> The smallest value that can be directly measured by a measuring instrument. Determines the **precision** of the instrument.
> 
> | Instrument | Typical Least Count |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | Metre scale | 1 mm |
> | Vernier calliper | 0.1 mm = 0.01 cm |
> | Screw gauge | 0.01 mm = 0.001 cm |

---

> [!note] **Light Year (ly)**
> The distance travelled by light in one year.
> $$1 \text{ ly} = c \times 1 \text{ year} = 3\times10^8 \times 3.156\times10^7 = 9.46 \times 10^{15} \text{ m}$$
> Used in astronomy. Not an SI unit.

---

## M

> [!note] **Metre (m)** *(All levels)*
> The SI base unit of **length**. Defined (since 1983, reaffirmed 2018) by fixing:
> $$c = 299{,}792{,}458 \text{ m s}^{-1} \text{ (exact)}$$

---

> [!note] **MKS System**
> A system based on **metre (m), kilogram (kg), and second (s)**. The precursor to the modern SI system.

---

> [!note] **Mole (mol)** *(Board/NEET/JEE)*
> The SI base unit of **amount of substance**. One mole contains exactly:
> $$N_A = 6.02214076 \times 10^{23} \text{ elementary entities}$$
> The entity must always be specified (atoms, molecules, ions, etc.).

---

## N

> [!note] **Nanometre (nm)**
> $$1 \text{ nm} = 10^{-9} \text{ m}$$
> Visible light range: 400–700 nm. Common in spectroscopy and nanotechnology.

---

## O

> [!info] **Order of Magnitude** *(Board/NEET)*
> The power of 10 when a number is in scientific notation and the coefficient is rounded:
> - Coefficient $\leq 5$ → round to 1
> - Coefficient $> 5$ → round to 10
> 
> Example: Earth's diameter $= 1.28 \times 10^7$ m → order of magnitude = **7**

---

## P

> [!note] **Parsec (pc)**
> A unit of astronomical distance.
> $$1 \text{ pc} = 3.086 \times 10^{16} \text{ m} \approx 3.26 \text{ light years}$$
> Defined as the distance at which 1 AU subtends an angle of 1 arcsecond.

---

> [!note] **Physical Quantity** *(All levels)*
> Any property that can be **measured**. Expressed as: (numerical value) × (unit).
> - **Scalar**: only magnitude (speed, temperature)
> - **Vector**: magnitude + direction (velocity, force)

---

> [!note] **Planck's Constant (h)**
> $$h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s} \quad \Rightarrow \quad [h] = [M L^2 T^{-1}]$$
> The fundamental quantum of action. Now defines the kilogram. Has the same dimensional formula as angular momentum.

---

> [!info] **Precision** *(Board/NEET)*
> The closeness of repeated measurements **to each other** (reproducibility). Does not imply closeness to the true value. A smaller least count → greater precision.
> 
> *Contrast with: Accuracy (closeness to true value)*

---

> [!note] **Pressure**
> Force per unit area. SI unit: pascal (Pa $= \text{N m}^{-2}$).
> $$[P] = [M L^{-1} T^{-2}]$$
> > ⚠️ Same dimensional formula as Stress, Young's modulus, and Energy density.

---

## R

> [!note] **Radian (rad)** *(Board)*
> The SI supplementary unit for **plane angle**.
> $$\theta \text{ (rad)} = \frac{\text{arc length}}{\text{radius}} = \frac{L}{L} \Rightarrow \text{dimensionless}$$
> 1 full circle $= 2\pi$ rad $= 360°$.

---

> [!note] **Relative Error** *(Board/NEET)*
> $$\text{Relative Error} = \frac{\Delta A}{\bar{A}} \times 100\%$$
> A smaller relative error indicates a more precise measurement.

---

> [!note] **Rounding Off** *(All levels)*
> Rules for reducing significant figures in a result:
> 
> | Digit to Drop | Action |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | $> 5$ | Raise preceding digit by 1 |
> | $< 5$ | Leave preceding digit unchanged |
> | $= 5$, preceding even | Drop (banker's rounding) |
> | $= 5$, preceding odd | Raise preceding digit by 1 |

---

## S

> [!note] **Scientific Notation** *(All levels)*
> Expressing a number as $N \times 10^n$ where $1 \leq N < 10$. Used for very large/small numbers and to unambiguously convey significant figures.

---

> [!note] **Second (s)** *(Board)*
> The SI base unit of **time**. Defined by fixing:
> $$\Delta\nu_{Cs} = 9{,}192{,}631{,}770 \text{ Hz}$$
> (caesium-133 ground-state hyperfine transition frequency)

---

> [!important] **Significant Figures** *(All levels — very important)*
> All certain digits in a measurement **plus the first uncertain (estimated) digit**. Indicate the precision of a measurement. Number of SF does **not** change with a change of units.

---

> [!note] **Solid Angle (steradian, sr)** *(Board/JEE)*
> The SI supplementary unit for 3D angle.
> $$d\Omega = \frac{dA}{r^2} \Rightarrow \text{dimensionless}$$
> 1 full sphere $= 4\pi$ sr.

---

> [!note] **Speed of Light (c)**
> $$c = 2.998 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1} \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$
> Used to define the metre. Dimensional formula: $[LT^{-1}]$.

---

> [!warning] **Systematic Error** *(Board/NEET)*
> An error that is **consistent and predictable**, affecting all measurements in the same way.
> 
> | Source | Example |
> |:---:|:---:|
> | Instrument calibration | Zero error in screw gauge |
> | Parallax error | Reading scale at angle |
> | Personal bias | Consistent early/late timing |
> 
> Can be **reduced but not eliminated by averaging**.

---

## T

> [!note] **True Value**
> The actual (theoretically exact) value of a physical quantity. In practice, estimated as the arithmetic mean of a large number of precise measurements.

---

## U

> [!note] **Unified Atomic Mass Unit (u)**
> $$1 \text{ u} = \frac{1}{12} \times \text{mass of }^{12}\text{C atom} = 1.66056 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$$
> Same as 'amu'. Used to express atomic and molecular masses.

---

> [!important] **Unit** *(All levels)*
> An arbitrarily chosen, internationally agreed-upon **standard** against which a physical quantity is measured. A good unit must be:
> - Well-defined and reproducible
> - Widely accessible
> - Invariant with time and environment

---

> [!note] **Unit Factor**
> A ratio equal to **1**, used in dimensional analysis to convert between units.
> $$\frac{1 \text{ km}}{1000 \text{ m}} = 1 \qquad \frac{1 \text{ h}}{3600 \text{ s}} = 1$$

---

## V

> [!note] **Vernier Calliper** *(Board/NEET — Measurement practical)*
> A precision measuring instrument with least count typically:
> $$LC = \frac{1 \text{ MSD} - 1 \text{ VSD}}{n} = 0.1 \text{ mm} = 0.01 \text{ cm}$$
> Uses a sliding vernier scale to improve precision beyond the main scale minimum division.

---

## W

> [!warning] **Weight vs. Mass**
> Weight = gravitational force on an object: $W = mg$
> 
> | Property | Weight | Mass |
> |:---:|:---:|:---:|
> | SI unit | Newton (N) | kilogram (kg) |
> | Dimensional formula | $[MLT^{-2}]$ | $[M]$ |
> | Location-dependent? | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
